~75% of metabolic energy exits as body heat — an unavoidable consequence of muscle thermodynamics (human muscles operate at roughly 25% mechanical efficiency at optimal cadence).

Temperature and air density. Air density ρ = P/(RsT) where T is absolute temperature and P falls exponentially with altitude via the barometric formula. At 0 °C, ρ ≈ 1.29 kg/m³; at 35 °C, ρ ≈ 1.15 kg/m³ — an 11% swing that feeds directly into aerodynamic power (Faero = ½ρCdAv²). Cold air is denser and harder to push through.

Note: temperature affects the metabolic efficiency figure in practice (cold muscles have slower enzyme kinetics; the body also burns additional calories via thermogenesis to maintain core temperature) but these effects are individual and not modelled here.

Power vs speed — stacked by loss type (dashed line = current speed)

Aerodynamic drag power scales as v³ — doubling speed requires eight times the power to overcome drag alone. Rolling resistance scales linearly with v. The crossover point where aero begins to dominate typically falls around 15–20 km/h, depending on CdA and Crr. This is why position and tyre choice have such different payoffs at different speeds.
